Short Bio

I am a PhD candidate at the Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto, advised by Professor Hans-Arno Jacobsen. My current research is at the core of distributed systems, especially consensus algorithms and fault tolerance. My research contributes to both the theoretical foundations of distributed computing and the design and implementation of practical systems. My long-term research goal is to develop 3H distributed systems -- characterized by high performance, high scalability, and high availability. It is focused on designing general and tailored consensus algorithms, fault tolerance, and consistency models to effectively support various distributed applications, including blockchains, databases, cloud computing, and distributed training systems.
